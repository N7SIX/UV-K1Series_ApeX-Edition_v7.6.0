# Documentation Update Summary — v7.6.5
---

## April 2026 Addendum 2: Peak Hold & Frequency Step Cap

- Added decaying peak hold feature to spectrum scan mode. `KEY_MENU` (previously a no-op in scan mode) now toggles peak hold on/off. Each display column tracks its historical maximum RSSI. When active, a dotted line (even pixel columns) is drawn above the live trace. Bins with no active signal decay at `PEAK_HOLD_DECAY_STEP = 9` RSSI units per sweep, giving approximately **5-second full fade**. The `PH` indicator appears in the spectrum status bar while active.
- Frequency shift step hard cap reduced from ±6000.00k to **±2500.00k**. First-boot fallback reduced from ±6000.00k to **±1000.00k**. EEPROM validation clamp updated to reject values above 250000 (restoring the new fallback). Prevents a single keypress from jumping most of the visible scan window.

**Impact:** Peak hold makes intermittent signals visible across multiple sweeps without cluttering the display indefinitely. The step cap makes frequency navigation proportional and controllable.

---

## April 2026 Addendum: Spectrum Control & Persistence Refinements

- Removed spectrum peak-hold overlay visuals for cleaner real-time trace readability.
- Frequency movement step now enforces a non-zero runtime floor (`100.00k`) to match persisted validation.
- Listening bandwidth toggle now applies to hardware immediately on key press.
- Spectrum startup now preserves user modulation selection (with safety sanitization), instead of forcing FM fallback.
- Added throttled dirty-settings autosave during runtime plus explicit dual-save on spectrum exit.

**Impact:** Improved control responsiveness, reduced user confusion, and stronger recovery of last-used spectrum settings after power cycles.
